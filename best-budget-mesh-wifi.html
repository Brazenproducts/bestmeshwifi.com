Best Budget Mesh WiFi Systems (Under $200)

Best Budget Mesh WiFi Systems (Under $200)

You don't need to spend $500 for whole-home WiFi. These budget picks prove it.

Updated April 2026 · By the BestMeshWiFi team

  1. TP-Link Deco X55 — Best Budget Overall
  2. Eero 6+ — Best Budget App Experience
  3. TP-Link Deco X20 — Best Under $120
  4. Google Nest WiFi (Non-Pro) — Best for Google Homes
  5. What You Give Up on a Budget

Here's a truth the networking industry doesn't want you to hear: for most households, a $150-200 mesh WiFi system works just fine. Unless you have gigabit internet, a 5,000 sq ft home, or 50+ connected devices, you don't need a $500 tri-band system.

The budget mesh market has gotten surprisingly good. WiFi 6 is standard even at low price points, and the apps have matured to the point where setup is painless. Here are the budget systems actually worth buying.

1. TP-Link Deco X55 — Best Budget Overall

Best Budget Pick

TP-Link Deco X55 (3-Pack)

★★★★½
~$160 for 3-pack

The Deco X55 is the budget mesh system we recommend most often. For around $160, you get a 3-pack WiFi 6 system that covers up to 6,500 sq ft. That's enough for most homes with room to spare.

It's a dual-band system, which means no dedicated backhaul channel. In practice, this matters less than you'd think — especially if your internet plan is under 500 Mbps. In our testing, the Deco X55 delivered 400+ Mbps in the same room and maintained 150-200 Mbps at the far end of a 2,800 sq ft home. That's plenty for streaming, video calls, and general use.

The Deco app is clean and easy to use, and TP-Link includes HomeCare security features (antivirus, parental controls, QoS) for free — no subscription required. That's a big deal when competitors charge $10/month for similar features.

Each unit has 3 Ethernet ports, which is generous for this price range. The units are compact and unobtrusive. Honestly, the only reason to spend more is if you need WiFi 6E or have a very demanding network.

2. Eero 6+ — Best Budget App Experience

Best App

Eero 6+ (3-Pack)

★★★★
~$200 for 3-pack

The Eero 6+ sits right at our $200 budget ceiling, but it earns its spot with the best app experience in the budget category. If you've ever been frustrated by a router's confusing interface, the Eero app will feel like a breath of fresh air.

Performance is comparable to the Deco X55 — it's a dual-band WiFi 6 system covering up to 4,500 sq ft with the 3-pack. Speeds are solid: 500+ Mbps close range, 180-250 Mbps at distance. The seamless roaming between nodes is excellent, inheriting the same smooth handoff technology from the pricier Eero Pro models.

The trade-off versus the Deco X55: slightly less coverage, fewer Ethernet ports (2 per unit vs. 3), and the advanced security features require an Eero Plus subscription. But if you value the app experience and plan to stay in the Eero ecosystem, it's worth the premium.

3. TP-Link Deco X20 — Best Under $120

Ultra Budget

TP-Link Deco X20 (3-Pack)

★★★★
~$110 for 3-pack

If you're really watching your budget, the Deco X20 is remarkable for the price. A 3-pack mesh WiFi 6 system for around $110 — that's less than many single routers. Coverage is rated at 5,800 sq ft, and while speeds are lower than the X55 (it's AX1800 vs. AX3000), it's still more than enough for internet plans up to 300 Mbps.

You get the same Deco app and free HomeCare features. The main sacrifice is speed — the X20 tops out around 300 Mbps in ideal conditions. For basic browsing, streaming, and video calls, that's fine. For gaming or large file transfers, step up to the X55.

4. Google Nest WiFi (Non-Pro) — Best Budget for Google Homes

Google Pick

Google Nest WiFi (Router + 2 Points)

★★★½
~$180 for 3-pack

The original Nest WiFi (not the Pro) is still available and has dropped to budget-friendly prices. The WiFi points double as Google Assistant speakers, which is a nice bonus if you're in the Google ecosystem. Coverage is decent at 5,400 sq ft for the 3-pack.

The catch: it's WiFi 5, not WiFi 6. That means lower maximum speeds and less efficient handling of multiple devices. For a household with basic internet needs and fewer than 20 devices, it works fine. But for the same money, the Deco X55 is objectively better on the networking side.

Buy this if the Google Assistant speakers add value for you. Otherwise, go with the TP-Link.

What You Give Up on a Budget

Budget mesh systems are genuinely good, but let's be honest about the trade-offs:

  • No dedicated backhaul. All budget systems are dual-band, meaning mesh traffic competes with your devices for bandwidth. This matters most in large homes or with many devices.
  • No WiFi 6E. The 6 GHz band is reserved for systems $250+. You won't miss it unless you have WiFi 6E devices and want the fastest possible local speeds.
  • Lower throughput. Budget systems typically max out at 300-500 Mbps real-world. If you have gigabit internet, you're leaving speed on the table.
  • Fewer Ethernet ports. Most budget units have 2-3 ports. Power users who hardwire lots of devices will need switches.
  • Less future-proofing. WiFi 7 is coming, and budget WiFi 6 systems won't support it. But WiFi 6 will be perfectly fine for years to come.

For most households — especially those with internet plans under 500 Mbps — these trade-offs don't matter in daily use. A $160 mesh system will transform your home WiFi experience just as dramatically as a $500 one.

The Bottom Line

The TP-Link Deco X55 is the budget mesh system to beat. It offers the best combination of coverage, performance, and features for the money. The Eero 6+ is worth the extra $40 if you want the best app experience. And the Deco X20 is a steal if you just need basic whole-home coverage.

Don't let anyone tell you that you need to spend $500 on mesh WiFi. These budget systems prove otherwise.
