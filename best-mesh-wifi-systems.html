We've been testing mesh WiFi systems for years now, and the market has finally matured to the point where there are genuinely great options at every price point. Gone are the days when you had to spend $700 to get reliable whole-home coverage.

That said, not every mesh system is created equal. Some are overpriced for what they deliver, some have terrible apps, and some just don't hold up when you've got 40+ devices hammering the network. Here's what actually works in 2026.

How We Test

We test every system in a real 2,800 sq ft home with multiple floors, thick walls, and a backyard that needs coverage. We measure throughput at various distances, test with 30+ connected devices, check latency for gaming, and live with each system for at least two weeks. We also factor in the app experience, because a powerful router with a terrible app is still a terrible experience.

1. Eero Pro 6E — Best Overall

#1 Pick

Eero Pro 6E (3-Pack)

★★★★★
~$450 for 3-pack

The Eero Pro 6E is our top pick for most people. It's not the absolute fastest system you can buy, and it's not the cheapest. But it nails the balance between performance, ease of use, and reliability better than anything else on the market.

Setup takes about 5 minutes through the Eero app. The tri-band WiFi 6E delivers excellent speeds throughout a typical home, and the system handles dozens of devices without breaking a sweat. Amazon's integration means you get built-in Alexa support and a surprisingly good parental controls system.

The main knock? You need an Eero Plus subscription ($9.99/mo) to unlock advanced security features and ad blocking. The base system works great without it, but it feels a bit nickel-and-dime for a $450 system.

2. Netgear Orbi 960 (RBKE963) — Best for Large Homes

Best for Large Homes

Netgear Orbi 960 (3-Pack)

★★★★½
~$550 for 3-pack

If you've got a big house — we're talking 4,000+ sq ft — the Orbi 960 is the system to beat. It's a quad-band WiFi 6E powerhouse with a dedicated backhaul channel that keeps your mesh network running at full speed even when every room is streaming 4K.

Raw throughput is the best we've tested. The Orbi consistently delivered 800+ Mbps at close range and maintained 300+ Mbps even two floors away. The 10 Gbps WAN port is future-proof for multi-gig internet plans.

The downsides: it's expensive, the units are large (they look like small vases), and the Orbi app, while improved, still isn't as polished as Eero's. But for sheer coverage and speed, nothing else comes close.

3. Google Nest WiFi Pro — Best for Smart Homes

Best Smart Home

Google Nest WiFi Pro (3-Pack)

★★★★
~$350 for 3-pack

Google's Nest WiFi Pro is a tri-band WiFi 6E system that integrates beautifully with the Google Home ecosystem. If you're already running Nest speakers, Chromecast, and Google Assistant throughout your home, this is the mesh system that ties it all together.

Performance is solid — not class-leading, but more than enough for most households. The compact, minimalist design blends into any room. And the Google Home app gives you a single place to manage your network alongside all your smart devices.

The trade-off is fewer advanced networking features. No dedicated backhaul band, limited QoS options, and no USB port for NAS. It's a system built for simplicity, not tinkering.

4. TP-Link Deco XE75 — Best Value

Best Value

TP-Link Deco XE75 (3-Pack)

★★★★½
~$300 for 3-pack

The Deco XE75 is the sweet spot of the mesh WiFi market. You get tri-band WiFi 6E with a dedicated backhaul channel — features that used to cost $500+ — for around $300. That's genuinely impressive.

Performance sits right between the Eero Pro 6E and the Nest WiFi Pro. Coverage is excellent for homes up to 5,500 sq ft (with the 3-pack), and the Deco app is clean and easy to use. TP-Link also includes HomeCare security features for free — no subscription required.

It's not as polished as the Eero ecosystem, and the units are a bit larger than we'd like. But dollar for dollar, this is the best mesh system you can buy right now.

5. Asus ZenWiFi ET8 — Best for Gaming

Best for Gaming

Asus ZenWiFi ET8 (2-Pack)

★★★★
~$400 for 2-pack

Gamers need low latency more than raw throughput, and the ZenWiFi ET8 delivers. Asus's adaptive QoS prioritizes gaming traffic automatically, and the dedicated 6 GHz backhaul keeps your mesh link from competing with your gaming devices.

The AiMesh system also gives you way more control than most mesh systems — VPN support, custom DNS, traffic analysis, and even AiProtection Pro security (powered by Trend Micro, free for life). If you're the kind of person who wants to actually configure your network, this is your system.

The 2-pack covers up to 5,500 sq ft, which is plenty for most homes. The app is functional but not as intuitive as Eero or Deco.

6. TP-Link Deco X55 — Best Budget

Best Budget

TP-Link Deco X55 (3-Pack)

★★★★
~$160 for 3-pack

Under $200 for a 3-pack mesh system with WiFi 6, and it actually works well? That's the Deco X55. It's a dual-band system, so you won't get the dedicated backhaul of pricier options, but for most households with standard internet speeds (under 500 Mbps), you won't notice the difference.

Coverage is rated at 6,500 sq ft for the 3-pack, and in our testing it reliably covered a 2,800 sq ft home with strong signal everywhere. The same Deco app and free HomeCare features apply here too.

If your internet plan is under 500 Mbps and you don't need WiFi 6E, this is the smart buy.

7. Linksys Velop MX5500 — Honorable Mention

Honorable Mention

Linksys Velop MX5500 (3-Pack)

★★★½
~$350 for 3-pack

The Velop MX5500 is a solid WiFi 6 tri-band system that does everything competently but nothing exceptionally. It's reliable, covers large homes well, and the Linksys app is decent. But at this price point, the Deco XE75 offers WiFi 6E for less money, and the Eero Pro 6E offers a better overall experience for a bit more.

We'd recommend the Velop if you find it on sale or if you're already in the Linksys ecosystem. Otherwise, the competition has passed it by.

The Bottom Line

For most people, the Eero Pro 6E is the mesh system to buy. It's the best combination of performance, simplicity, and reliability. If you're on a budget, the TP-Link Deco X55 is a steal. And if you've got a mansion to cover, the Netgear Orbi 960 is worth the investment.

Whatever you choose, any of these systems will be a massive upgrade over a single router trying to cover your whole home. Mesh WiFi isn't a gimmick — it's the way home networking should work.
